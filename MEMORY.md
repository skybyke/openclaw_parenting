## Strategies Tried

### What's Worked
<!-- Format: Strategy — Context — Why it worked -->
- **"Drop the rope"** — When pulling harder triggers more resistance, step back. Archer eventually moves when the pressure releases. Never truly backfired.
- **"3 more minutes or I'm packing breakfast to go"** — Clear countdown with natural consequence. Puts choice on him, avoids nagging spiral.
- **CICO system** — Check-in/check-out with teacher initials each period. Immediate daily reward (candy), weekly reward (takeout choice), 2-month reward (metalworking/forging class). Hit 80% threshold in first 2 weeks.
- **Default-deny network** — Blocking everything except allowlisted school sites. Reduced game-playing in class significantly. He tests for gaps but schoolwork becomes easier path.
- **Weekly cooking with a parent** — Archer chooses the meal, one-on-one time. First meal: cooked carrots and spaghetti. Channels his interest in fire/making things constructively.

### What Hasn't Worked
<!-- Format: Strategy — Context — Why it failed -->
- **Device removal alone** — Removing laptop from school (March 4-5) didn't improve CICO completion. Still hit only 50% both days. Problem isn't just the device.
